Studets about Yogacharya

Introduction:
The article is about “yoga” in International Information Institute of Technology, Hyderabad highlighting the student response to the same.
Yoga is currently conducted as a compulsory 20 day course in which Yogacharya Vinayak ji introduces us to the novel practice of “yoga”, giving us blend of theory and practical lessons.

Article:
As I went through the student responses, I found a pattern, well a “changing” pattern. When we enter the college with huge dreams and hopes of becoming successful computer science engineers, filled with excitement, the minds conditioned by careless seniors who themselves have not yet passed the course of “yoga”, about the same , making the course seem like a burden and the concept non-sense.
At first students were repulsed by the fact that they had to wake up as early as 6:30 am in the morning, the strictness of Yogacharya Sir and the initially tough appearing “asanas” and the theory lessons that seem to be boring in the beginning.
Fighting with the C programming assignments, the problems in DLP, coping up with the heavy academic load, most of the first years give up and fell in the complacency of completing it the next year.
The first few days are the most difficult but those who survive the first week realize the benefits of “yoga” and the power it beholds. It requires patience and immense confidence to overcome initial hardships so that I can feel the effects of “asanas” and mediation.
As those who chose to fight and stay got their share of benefits. As the student response record show most of the students were experiences a huge increase in flexibility and stamina. They experienced relaxation and peace of mind, especially the seniors who were attending the classes in the evening.
Meditation helped them increase their concentration and hence their academic performance. Waking up early in the morning and performing the “pranayamas” and “asanas” help them to stay awake in the classes and get the most out of it. They feel less tired and increasingly alert throughout the day and slept on proper time, following their scheduled perfectly.
Some of them found the activity of noticing and observing their thoughts as they breathe to be immensely freshening and enjoyable. This exercise is very basic to the concept of knowing one’s own self. “Yoga helps us grow spiritually by turning it inwards.” .Those who came in the evening wrote that they felt fresh after a hard day’s work.
The “yog nidra pranayama” was found by many to be very effective to calm down the mind. Thinking about oneself, mutual games and exercises like the “X-Y” and building up energy using one's own palm was appreciated by many. Students achieved increased control over their breath and reduced sleep hours.
All in all “yoga” made the students realize an entirely new meaning of their life and a peace of mind. Chanting of “omkara” was especially appreciated by the students and mentioned that it filled them with positive energy. “Shavasana” the favorite “asana” of every student brought them to feel vibrations of mind and body, resting completely and peacefully, connecting with inner-self.
The “asana” of “surya namaskara” the practice was followed traditionally and was believed to be a complete exercise i.e. an exercise for the entire body proved beneficial to everyone. The laughter exercise was extremely fun filled and energizing.
People with bronchitis were benefited by the “pranayamas”. Sound therapy was effective in lowering anxiety. A student suffering with the problem of stomach reflux for 4 years, upon learning and practicing the technique of “vajrasan” relieved him entirely of the disease.
People suffering from cines, a disease with continuous flow of watery or semi-solid substance through the nose was cured under the guidance of our Yogacharya who taught and made the patient practice the exercise of “neti” which cured him. He developed a proper package for people suffering from back pain and many benefited from the same.
“Asanas” involving slow folding of the knee helped a student to recover from ACL injury. Students found philosophical discussions with sir very enlightening. A student having extremely bad pain during her periods from her lower abdomen to her toes and irregular periods, sir made her go through a set of “asanas” which resulted in almost painless and regular periods.
“Yoga” reduces weight and naturopathy helps improving fairness making the patient look bright all throughout the day. The power of the practice is beyond the scope of this article, naturopathy can cure diseases that today’s allopathic medicine cannot dream to cure. Finally students, who were made to do “yoga” forcefully, realize the benefits.
Many recommended it for all, further said that they like to continue it in the future. Many said that the morning sessions were much more beneficial and suggested that “yoga” classes should be taken throughout the semester. Along with me, many others thanked the strict, highly disciplined but lovable Yogacharya sir.
