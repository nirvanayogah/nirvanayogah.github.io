Crisis management:

Crisis is an emergency situation in everyone’s life which causes embarrassment.
Crisis can be categorized into two types:
  1. Avoidable crisis: It is caused due to habit/personality traits, bad manners, addiction, Deferring and avoidance of responsibilities.
  2. Unavoidable crisis: It is caused due to limitation of knowledge and ability.
In Crisis we get depressed which leads to loss of efficiency and effectiveness. In turn this depression causes fear and stress.

What will be taught in this session? :
  • How and why to change attitude to handle real life situations?
  • Realizing importance of Self-respect
  • How to command respect instead of demanding?
  • Yogic techniques like sukshma vyayam, kriya, asana, breathing techniques, chanting, and meditation for immediate, medium and long term relief. Some techniques take less than two minutes.
  • Yogic technique like “happiness analysis” stops the negative impacts of stress and improves the happiness, peace and harmony.
Stress management:

Stress is a very common event and experience in every one’s life. According to a recent study, almost 90% people experience high levels of stress. Not only it is today’s No.1 Killer in the world but also causes tremendous loss in terms of productivity, money and creativity.

When capacity is less than demand leads to stress.
Stress = capacity < demand

The causes of stress are internal and external like:
  • Illness
  • Personality traits
  • Environmental factors
  • Daily hassles
  • Social interaction
  • Events

Stress is part of our body’s natural response of “fight or flight” to protect ourselves from external factors.
In olden days, stress was short lasting which allowed a human to grow and survive but now-a-days stress is a long lasting or even a continuous process, which results in detrimental effect on body and mind. Stress then manifests into acute/chronic diseases and eventually leads to death.

There have been many methods of relief and adaptation from stressful situations. These methods are not that effective to handle such immense stress. In fact, Stress is still rising especially amongst youngsters in the name of competition and success.

In Yoga, there is a holistic approach which takes into account of every dimension of human personality like physical, pranic (vital energy), manasic (Psychological), intellectual, social and above all spiritual. This helps man attain bliss (Ananda / happiness).

These yogic learnings will attempt to arm you with the most powerful anti-stress weapon known to man … “Ananda”

Improvement of sixth sense:
We experience the world with the help of our five sense organs those are namely Eyes, ears, nose, Skin, and tongue. Mind analyses these experiences and then correlates it with previous experiences. During to this process, some understanding is developed which determines how we perceive the experience either as good or bad.

In either of the cases our mind gets involved in planning about how to retain or achieve the good experience and how to get rid of bad experience/situation. These understandings prejudice us to consider the experience to be good or bad making us a conditioned person.

For Example, when we look at cow some people may consider it as god, some people may perceive as an object to be eaten. So object is same but the perception is different because of our conditioning.
Developing the sixth sense helps us free ourselves from the clutches of five senses as well as the related prejudice which is created because of the previous experiences.

In our workshops, most of the people can experience the sixth sense in the very first session. One can practice and become the master of sixth sense which can help a person to take right decisions at the right time.

As a matter of fact, the incidence of cancer has been attributed to the stress of modern life. It has been proved by researchers all over the world that laughter is a much easier form of stress busting.

It has long been recognized that stress weakens the immune system, leaving you more vulnerable to illness. You have been probably noticed yourself that you simply feel better after a good belly laugh.

This class includes playful interactive group laughter exercises with stretching. This session teaches you how to laugh whole-heartily and provides you a safe space for you to tap in your inner child by letting go of judgment, negativity, hostility, fear, and stress and replacing these feelings with love, peace, acceptance and joy.

Process of laughter therapy: Laughter Therapy deals with un-conditional laughter, combining with yogic breathing. Anyone can laugh without relying on humor, jokes or comedy. Laughter is initially simulated as a physical exercise while maintaining eye contact with others in the group and promoting childlike playfulness. In most cases, this process leads to real and contagious laughter.

Science has proven that the body cannot differentiate between simulated and real laughter.

  • It reduces levels of the stress hormones
